Miyako

The Miyako Pony (宮古馬) is a rare native breed from Miyako Island in southern Japan, with fewer than 50 individuals remaining. Believed to date back to at least the 13th century, these ponies have been bred in the region for centuries and are designated as a prefectural natural treasure.

Tokara

The Tokara Pony (トカラ馬) is a rare and endangered breed native to the Tokara Islands in southern Japan. Historically used for farming and transportation, these small but sturdy ponies are an important part of Japan's agricultural past and cultural heritage.

Dosanko

The Dosanko (道産子 ) also known as the Hokkaido Washu, is a heritage breed from northern Japan. Believed to be descended from horses brought from the south during the Edo period. These horses have adapted to the extreme cold and snow, making them a tough and rugged breed.

Nooitgedachter

The Nooitgedachter is a rare South African breed with only approximately 700 individuals remaining on the stud book. The breed's history begins over 300 years ago when Dutch settlers brought horses—a mix of Javan ponies, Barb, Arab, and Spanish bloodlines—to the Cape in 1653.

SA Vlaamperd

The SA Vlaamperd is a rare and versatile South African breed known for its elegant movement, strength, and willing temperament. Originally developed from imported Friesian stallions crossed with local stock, the breed became especially valued as a driving horse. Vlaams are versatile horses.

The Criollo Horse descends from Spanish horses brought to South America in the 16th century. Over centuries of natural selection, it developed exceptional stamina, hardiness, and intelligence, thriving on open plains and in harsh climates.

Galiceno

Described as “A Small Horse with a Big Heart,” Galicenos are giving horses that love to please the people they are with. Standing between 12 and 13.2 hands high, these small, sturdy horses have quite the story. They were originally brought to the Americas and Hispaniola by Cortés and the conquistadors, continuing to breed freely in southern Mexico into the mid-1900s.

Waler

The Waler Horse, named after its origins in New South Wales, is a hardy and versatile breed forged from a mix of Timor, Welsh, Percheron, Clydesdale, Suffolk, Cleveland Bay, Thoroughbred, and Arabian bloodlines. Known for their courage, intelligence, and steady temperament, Walers excel in trail riding and were renowned cavalry horses during World War I and other conflicts.

Kaimanawa

The Kaimanawa horse is a wild horse from New Zealand. Originating from ponies such as Exmoors and Welsh that were released in the late 1800s, these horses have adapted to the rugged environment of New Zealand’s central North Island. Over time, other breeds, including Thoroughbred, Arabian, Standardbred, and Clydesdale, were introduced, contributing to the genetic diversity and unique traits of the modern Kaimanawa.

Caspian

Early Persian artifacts depict animals of normal size next to unusually small and refined horses. An an ancient breed, once thought to be extinct, until one woman rediscovered them in 1965. Louise Firouz, an American horsewoman found a small dark bay stallion pulling a cart near the Caspian sea in northern Iran. It was the start of her lifelong quest to save the breed.

Noriker

The Noriker is one of the oldest draft horse breeds in Europe, originating from the Roman province of Noricum, which largely corresponds to modern-day Austria. These horses are known for their gentle temperament, versatility, and distinctive coat colors, ranging from solid to various patterns.

Andalusian

The Andalusian, or Pura Raza Española (PRE), is a historic Spanish horse breed renowned for its elegance, versatility, and athleticism. Originating from the Iberian Peninsula, these horses have been carefully refined since the 16th century, combining beauty with strength for use in warfare, bullfighting, and classical dressage. Known for their expressive movements, intelligence, and strong connection with their riders.
